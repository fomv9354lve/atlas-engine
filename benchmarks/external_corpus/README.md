# External-validation corpus

Atlas evaluated on circuits that **others** published or assumed hard — **not** our
self-generated corpus. The goal is to test the routing verdict against the field's own
"this needs a QPU" intuition: quantum-advantage circuits plus large published
algorithm / benchmark circuits.

This directory is fully reproducible. Each circuit ships as OpenQASM under `qasm/`
(or is regenerated by the runner scripts), and the verdicts are in the three
`results.json` files. No secrets, no tokens — only circuits and their routing output.

## What ran

| File | Tier | Circuits |
|------|------|----------|
| `results.json` | Tier 1 — advantage + published algorithm circuits | 18 |
| `tier2_vqe/results.json` | Tier 2 — VQE / UCCSD ansätze (HEA + chemistry) | 12 |
| `tier2_qaoa_trotter/results.json` | Tier 2 — QAOA + Trotter dynamics | 16 |

**Consolidated (recomputed from the three files): 46 circuits.**

- **41 routed to a classical tier** (CPU 38 · TENSOR 2 · HPC_FIRST 1).
- **3 escalated** — out of classical reach: `sycamore_rcs_53q_depth8`,
  `sycamore_rcs_53q_depth20`, `kicked_ising_127q_hard_5steps`.
- **2 did not complete in budget** (honest TIMEOUTs, 600 s): `vqe_uccsd_n28`,
  `qaoa_dense_n20_p2`.

The honest punch line: Atlas does **not** rubber-stamp everything classical. It
**escalated the genuine advantage frontier** (Sycamore RCS at supremacy depth; the
*hard* IBM-utility kicked-Ising point), while the *easy / Clifford* kicked-Ising
control point routed CLASSICAL — matching the field's own finding that the easy regime
is classically reachable and the hard regime is not. And `multiplier_n45` has T-count
2,646 yet routes CPU at MPS bond 1: **magic ≠ hardness**.

"Classical tier" does **not** mean "laptop-trivial" — several are TENSOR/HPC-reachable.
The honest meaning is that methods the field assumed were QPU-only (large tensor-network
/ statevector / stabilizer runs) reach them.

## Sources of the circuits

- **Sycamore random circuit sampling (RCS)** — the 2019 "quantum supremacy" family.
  Arute et al., *Nature* 573, 505 (2019). Later classically reproduced post-hoc:
  Pan & Zhang, *PRL* 129, 090502 (2022); Liu et al., Gordon Bell 2021. Atlas escalates
  it (correct — genuinely high-treewidth at depth).
- **IBM 127-qubit "utility" kicked-Ising** — Kim et al., *Nature* 618, 500 (2023).
  Classically reproduced by Tindall, Fishman, Stoudenmire & Sels, *PRX Quantum* 5,
  020332 (2024); Begušić & Chan (2024); and others via tensor networks. We include
  three points: the *hard* point (escalates), a *Clifford* control point (routes
  classical, Stim poly-time, Aaronson–Gottesman 2004), and a 20-step variant.
- **Algorithm / benchmark circuits** — QASMBench-style and MQT-Bench-style families:
  adder, multiplier, GHZ-127, cat, Bernstein–Vazirani, QFT, Quantum Volume, kNN/DNN
  (QML), QuGAN. Constructed/obtained as representative instances at the stated sizes.
- **VQE / UCCSD** (`tier2_vqe/`) — hardware-efficient ansätze (linear + full
  entanglement) and chemistry UCCSD-style circuits (H2, LiH, BeH2).
- **QAOA + Trotter** (`tier2_qaoa_trotter/`) — QAOA-MaxCut (line + dense) and Trotterized
  TFIM / Heisenberg dynamics on line and grid topologies.

## Reproduce

```bash
# Tier 1 (advantage + algorithm circuits)
python3 run_corpus.py        # main harness; run_phase2.py for the advantage points

# Tier 2
python3 tier2_vqe/gen_and_run.py
python3 tier2_qaoa_trotter/run_corpus.py
```

Engine used: `cost_atlas` + `route_adjudicator` (the same REAL estimators as the main
corpus — magic / MPS-bond / treewidth / stabilizer, min over methods). This was an
offline research harness; nothing was deployed by it.

## Honest caveats

- These are constructed/obtained representative circuits, not a harvested production
  trace. They are size-faithful to the cited works but not bit-identical to every
  original.
- "Classical tier" ≠ "trivial" — see above.
- 2 circuits timed out at 600 s and are reported as incomplete, not as classical.
- Numbers here do not generalize beyond this set; this is a validation probe against
  the field's intuition, not a universal claim.
